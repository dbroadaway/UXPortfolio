About Me

Design is essentially problem solving. I like to understand as much about the challenge as possible before iterating on a UX solution. Deep knowledge of the problem informs every aspect of my design process from creation through each design iteration. User research can be challenging. I have conducted user tests at coffee shops, getting quick feedback for the price of a gift card. The UX wall at a user conference allowed me to pull users in for a quick interview. Steve Krug’s DIY user testing process was part of the sprint cycle. Sales, customer service and support departments can always provide direct user feedback.

Coco Chanel famously said, “before you leave the house, look in the mirror and take at least one thing off.” I take the same approach to UX and Product Design. Core features should be obvious and immediately available. Ancillary features should be easy find to but should not obstruct core features. Visual Design should enhance the experience without being disruptive. I always ask myself is this necessary, useful, or valuable?

Product design must be informed by user needs, technical limitations, business constraints, and efficient process. As a product designer I put myself in the shoes of the user, the client, the developer, and other stakeholders as I advocate for the most pragmatic solution to a design problem.
